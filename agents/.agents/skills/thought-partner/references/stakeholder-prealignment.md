The real decision happens before the meeting, not in it. Meetings are ceremonies to adopt decisions — not arenas where decisions get made. If you walk in expecting to win with data and slides, you've already lost if the opposition has done their groundwork.

This is Nemawashi: systematic one-on-one pre-alignment with every stakeholder before the group convenes.

## How It Works

For each person who will be in the room:

1. **Share your proposal privately first.** Let them read and react without an audience. People don't think well in public — they anchor to the first opinion, defend status, and rarely admit uncertainty in front of peers.
2. **Surface objections in private.** Hear the real concerns (political, personal, practical) that people won't voice in a group setting.
3. **Adapt the proposal based on what you hear.** If three people raise the same objection, it's a real problem — fix it before the meeting, not during.
4. **Confirm alignment individually.** After adaptation, check that the key stakeholders are on board.

The meeting itself becomes ratification, not debate.

## Who to Pre-align

Identify both the formal decision makers and the informal ones — the trusted advisors each manager consults privately. Convincing a trustee converts the manager for free.

Map stakeholders by what they care about:
- **PMs** → scope, predictability, gates they can use to manage risk
- **Engineers** → environment quality, no legacy baggage, chances to clean up messes
- **Executives** → total cost of ownership, speed to market

Same proposal. Three different conversations.

## Handling Resistance

For immovable people: propose a pilot with an honest rollback condition. If it fails, roll back and admit you were wrong. This is the credibility move — pilots without rollback conditions are just delayed mandates.

If you don't honor the rollback, you lose all credibility permanently.

## NEVER

- **NEVER** walk into a decision meeting without having spoken to every stakeholder individually first. **Why:** the first person to speak sets the anchor; if that person opposes you and you haven't pre-aligned, you're fighting uphill in front of an audience. **Instead:** do the work in one-on-ones. The meeting is the finish line, not the starting line.
- **NEVER** run a pilot without a pre-committed rollback condition. **Why:** a pilot without rollback is a delayed mandate — people know this, and it erodes trust before the pilot starts. **Instead:** state the rollback criteria upfront. Honor them. This is what buys you the next pilot.
